Research Themes and Projects

I study how life responds to environmental change across modern and geologic timescales. My research bridges paleobiology, ecology, and evolution to test how morphology, symbiosis, and nutrient/contaminant cycling shape biodiversity and ecosystem resilience.

About.

Chemosymbiosis in Lucinid Bivalves

Data: Lucinid bivalves represent one of the most taxonomically and ecologically diverse groups of chemosymbiotic animals, inhabiting shallow coastal habitats from seagrass meadows to mangrove sediments. My research examines how lucinid morphology, symbiotic partnerships, and habitat geochemistry co-evolve across ecological and geologic time. Using geometric morphometrics, phylogenetics, and sedimentary geochemistry, I investigate how lucinids and their sulfur-oxidizing symbionts contribute to nutrient cycling and ecosystem stability in both modern and fossil systems. Aquatic Ecology & Wetland Restoration

About.

Project: Kootenai River Floodplain Reconnection

Kootenai River: Seasonal floodplain reconnection is expected to rebalance N:P, increase in-situ production, and export lower-trophic biomass and balanced nutrients to the mainstem.

Project: PFAS Treatment Train

PFAS: Pilot-scale electrochemical oxidation upstream of ion exchange, addressing field-deployable remediation of aqueous film-forming foam (AFFF) contaminants.
